Intro – Has Anyone Ever Been Told Not To Do Something Good?
5 min

Get the children thinking about staying faithful before the story begins.

Ask the group: “Has anyone ever been told NOT to do something good — like praying, or being kind, or telling the truth?” (Let them share — affirm every response, big or small.)
Ask: “What would you do if someone said you weren’t allowed to talk to God anymore?” (Take a few answers.)
Ask: “Do you think it’s ever hard to keep doing the right thing, even when no one else is?” (Encourage a few responses.)
Leader note: Today we’re going to meet a man named Daniel. He worked for a king in a faraway land, far from his home. Some jealous men tried to get him in trouble — they even made it against the law for him to pray to God. But Daniel chose to stay faithful to God anyway, no matter what it cost him.
🎮
Game – Daniel Story Match-Up
5 min

Print the A3 outline page and stick it up where everyone can reach it. Each child gets a coloured cut-out piece and has to find and stick it onto the matching outline using a small piece of Blu Tack (or similar).

How to Play

Hand out one coloured character piece per child — Daniel, the angel, the king, the soldier, the jealous officials, and the lions all appear on the sheet. Children take turns finding their shape on the A3 outline page and sticking their piece on top of it.

  • If you have more children than pieces, print a second copy of the colour sheet and cut out extra pieces so everyone gets a turn.
  • For younger children, talk through who each character is as they place their piece — it’s a great way to preview the story before the Bible lesson.

Printable Game Pieces

Print the A3 outline page once per game. Print the colour sheet and cut out one set of pieces per child (extra copies if needed).

Bible Lesson – Daniel and the Lions’ Den
10 min

Bible passage: Daniel 6

Watch the video together, then work through the discussion questions below.

▶︎ Watch the Bible Story Video

The Story in Brief

Daniel was one of the king’s most trusted helpers, and he prayed to God three times every day. Some jealous officials wanted Daniel in trouble, so they tricked the king into making a law that said no one could pray to anyone except the king himself — anyone who disobeyed would be thrown into a den of lions. Daniel kept praying to God anyway. He was thrown into the lions’ den, but God sent an angel to shut the lions’ mouths, and Daniel wasn’t hurt at all. The king was overjoyed and praised God for saving Daniel.

Jesus Connection: Daniel stayed faithful to God even when it was dangerous, and God rescued him from the lions. Jesus stayed faithful to God too — all the way to the cross — but Jesus wasn’t rescued from death the way Daniel was rescued from the lions. Instead, Jesus went through death itself to rescue us from sin, and then rose again. Daniel trusted God to save his life; we can trust Jesus to save our lives forever. He is the ultimate Standing Strong Hero.

Craft – Daniel’s Lion
10 min

Children cut out a lion head and mane, curl the mane pieces, and glue them onto the lion’s body to make their own friendly lion from the den.

Before the Session

  • Print the lion body, head, and mane templates onto A4 cardstock — one set per child.
  • Pre-cut a few sample sets so children can see what the finished lion should look like.

Materials

  • Printed lion body template — one per child
  • Printed lion head and mane template — one per child
  • Crayons or markers
  • Scissors
  • Glue sticks
  • Pencil (for curling the mane pieces)

Steps

  1. Children colour the lion body and the lion head and mane.
  2. Cut out the lion head and mane shape.
  3. Carefully curl back each of the long mane pieces just behind the lion’s head — rolling them around a pencil works well to get a nice curl.
  4. Glue the finished head and mane onto the lion’s body.
Tip: Curling the mane pieces takes a little practice — show the children how to roll the paper around a pencil and hold it for a few seconds before letting go.
Leader note: As children finish their lions, ask them to retell what happened in the lions’ den — it’s a great low-pressure way to recap the Bible story while they work.
