Intro – Am I Too Small?
5 min

Get the children thinking about size and strength before the story begins.

Ask the group: “Who is the biggest person in the room right now?” (Let them look around and answer.)
Ask: “Do you think the biggest person always wins?” (Take a few answers.)
Ask: “Has anyone ever felt too small or not strong enough to do something?” (Encourage a few responses.)
Leader note: Today we’re going to meet someone in the Bible who was small — but God used him to do something amazing. His name was David, and he faced a giant!
📖
Bible Lesson – David and Goliath
10 min

Bible passage: 1 Samuel 17

Watch the video together, then work through the discussion questions below.

▶︎ Watch the Bible Story Video

Discussion Questions

1. Why was everyone so scared of Goliath?
Sample answer: Because he was enormous — a giant soldier with big armour and weapons. No one thought they could beat him.
2. Why wasn’t David scared, even though he was just a boy?
Sample answer: David trusted God. He knew God had helped him before — when he fought lions and bears to protect his sheep — and he believed God would help him again.
3. What does this story tell us about God?
Sample answer: God doesn’t need us to be the biggest or the strongest. When we trust in him, he can do things we could never do on our own.

Jesus Connection: David was just a small shepherd boy, but he wasn’t afraid because he knew God was with him. God used someone that everyone else overlooked to do something amazing — defeating a giant that no soldier dared to face. We are a bit like the Israelites: we have an enemy we can’t beat on our own — sin separates us from God and there is nothing we can do to fix that ourselves. But Jesus is our champion. Just like David stepped forward when no one else could, Jesus stepped forward for us. He fought our battle by dying on the cross in our place, taking the punishment we deserved. When we trust in Jesus, we are saved — not by our own strength or goodness, but by his.

🙏 Prayer

Dear God, thank you that you are always with us — just like you were with David. Thank you that you don’t need us to be big or strong or brave on our own. Help us to trust you when things feel too hard or too scary. And thank you most of all for Jesus, our champion, who fought the biggest battle for us and won. Help us to put our trust in him. Amen.

Craft – David & Goliath Cup Launcher
10 min

Children build their own cup launcher — which they’ll use in the game!

Before the Session

  • Print the David picture (one sheet gives two copies — cut in half, one per child).
  • Cut the bottom out of each paper cup using a craft knife.
  • Cut the rounded end off each balloon (leave the tied end intact — that’s what kids pull back to launch).

Materials (per child)

  • Printed David picture (see below)
  • Paper cup with the bottom cut out (pre-cut before the session)
  • Large balloon with the rounded end cut off (tied end remains intact for pulling)
  • Sticky tape
  • Glue

Steps

  1. Give each child their prepared paper cup (bottom already cut out).
  2. Glue the David picture around the outside of the cup.
  3. Stretch the balloon (cut/open end first) over the bottom opening of the cup.
  4. Secure the balloon tightly with sticky tape around the rim.
  5. Your launcher is ready — hold it by the cup, drop a ping pong ball in through the top opening, pull back the tied end of the balloon, and let go!

Game – Knock Down Goliath!
5 min

Use the launchers the children just made to knock down Goliath!

Setup

  1. Place one or more Goliath cylinders on a flat surface or low table at one end of the room.
  2. Mark a firing line with tape on the floor — about 1–2 metres from Goliath.
  3. Give each child a ping pong ball and their cup launcher.

How to Play

  • Children take turns standing at the firing line and launching their ping pong ball to knock Goliath down.
  • If you have multiple Goliath cylinders set up, children can all launch at the same time.
  • Cheer together when Goliath falls — just like the Israelites cheered for David!
  • Reset and let everyone have another turn if time allows.
