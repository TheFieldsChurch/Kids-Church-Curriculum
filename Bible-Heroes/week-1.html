Intro – Has Anyone Ever Been Asked to Do Something Really Weird?
5 min

Get the children thinking about trust and obedience before the story begins.

Ask the group: “Has anyone ever been asked to do something that seemed really weird or didn’t make sense?” (Let them share — affirm every response.)
Ask: “What would you think if someone told you to build a giant boat — with no ocean nearby?” (Take a few answers.)
Ask: “Would you still do it if you knew it was God asking you?” (Encourage a few responses.)
Leader note: Today we’re going to meet a man named Noah. Everyone around him thought he was crazy — but Noah kept building, kept obeying, and kept trusting God. Let’s find out what happened.
🎮
Game – Animal Charades
5 min

Children take turns picking a card and acting out the animal on it — no sounds allowed! The rest of the group guesses.

How to Play

  1. Shuffle the printed animal cards face-down in a pile.
  2. One child picks a card — no peeking — and acts out that animal without making any sounds.
  3. The rest of the class calls out their guesses. First correct guess wins a turn!
  4. Keep going until every child has had a turn, or until time is up.
Tie-back: After the game say — “All of these animals had to trust Noah to keep them safe on the ark — just like we need to trust our Super Saviour Jesus to keep us safe!”

Bible Lesson – Noah and the Ark
10 min

Bible passage: Genesis 6–9

Watch the video together, then work through the discussion questions below.

▶︎ Watch the Bible Story Video

The Story in Brief

  • The world had become a very sad place. People were doing wrong things and not following God.
  • Noah was different — he loved God and tried to do what was right, even when no one around him did.
  • God asked Noah to build a huge ark and Noah obeyed straight away, even though it probably seemed very strange to everyone else.
  • It rained for 40 days and 40 nights — but God kept Noah, his family, and the animals completely safe inside the ark.
  • When the rain stopped, God put a rainbow in the sky as a sign of His promise: “I will never flood the whole earth like this again.”
  • God always keeps His promises. Noah trusted God, and God was faithful.

Discussion Questions

Why did God ask Noah to build the ark?
Sample answer: Because people were doing lots of wrong things and God wanted to keep Noah and his family safe because Noah loved Him.
How do you think Noah felt building the ark when no one else believed him?
Sample answer: Maybe scared or lonely — but he kept going because God told him to. He trusted God even when it was hard.
What does the rainbow remind us about God?
Sample answer: God made a promise and He always keeps His promises! We can trust what God says.

Jesus Connection: Noah trusted God and was saved through the ark. The Bible teaches that all of us have sinned and turned away from God — but God loves us so much He sent His Son Jesus to take the punishment for our sin on the cross. Just as there was one way of escape through the ark, there is one way of salvation: through Jesus alone. Noah needed the ark to be saved. We need Jesus. He is the ultimate Super Saviour.

🙏 Prayer

Lord, thank you that you are a God who keeps every promise. Help us to trust you like Noah did — even when things don’t make sense, even when others laugh, even when it’s hard. Thank you most of all for Jesus, our Super Saviour, who took the punishment for our sin so that we could be saved. Amen.

Craft – Noah’s Ark Spinner
10 min

Children make a two-sided spinning wheel showing the stormy sea transforming into a rainbow sky — a reminder that God keeps His promises.

Materials

  • Printed spinner template — A4 cardstock (one per child)
  • Scissors
  • Split pins — one per child

Steps

  1. Print the template onto A4 cardstock. Cut out the circle (with the rainbow and storm) and the ark shape.
  2. Place the ark picture on top of the circle so the bottom edges line up and the dot is centred.
  3. Push the split pin through the centre dot of both pieces, then splay the pin flat at the back to hold them together.
  4. Spin the wheel to go from the stormy sky to the rainbow. Talk about how God changed Noah’s situation — from scary to wonderful.
Craft talk: As children spin their wheels, say — “When you spin from the storm to the rainbow, remember — God turned Noah’s scary storm into something beautiful. And one day, Jesus will make everything new and beautiful for us too. He’s our Super Saviour who turns sadness into joy!”
