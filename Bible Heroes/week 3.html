Intro – Has Anyone Ever Been Scared to Be Brave?
5 min

Get the children thinking about courage before the story begins.

Ask the group: “Has anyone ever had to say something brave, even when they were scared? What happened?” (Let them share — affirm every response, big or small.)
Ask: “Do you think brave people ever feel scared?” (Take a few answers.)
Ask: “What if you had to do something really scary to help a whole lot of people?” (Encourage a few responses.)
Leader note: Today we’re going to meet a girl named Esther. She was an orphan — she had no mum or dad — and she was raised by her older cousin Mordecai. But something amazing happened: Esther became a queen! And then God asked her to do the bravest thing she had ever done.
🎮
Game – Brave or Scared?
5 min

Mark one side of the room “Brave!” and the other “Scared!” — a sign, a piece of paper on the floor, or just call it out. Children start in the middle.

How to Play

The leader reads out a scenario. Children jump to the “Brave!” or “Scared!” side based on what they think they would do. Remind the kids: there’s no right or wrong answer here — we all get scared sometimes, and that’s okay!

Scenarios

  • A friend is being left out at lunchtime. Do you go and sit with them?
  • Someone says something that isn’t true. Do you say “that’s not right”?
  • You break something by accident. Do you tell the truth?
  • A new kid at church doesn’t know anyone. Do you go say hi?
  • Someone is being mean to a smaller child. Do you get a grown-up?
  • Your friend wants you to leave someone out of a game. Do you say no?
  • You see someone drop all their things and no one stops to help. Do you stop?
  • Someone asks you if you go to church. Do you say yes?
  • You did something wrong and your parents don’t know yet. Do you tell them?
  • A kid at school is being teased and everyone is laughing. Do you stand next to them?

Printable Signs

Print both signs (A4, one per page) and place them on opposite sides of the room before the session.

Bible Lesson – Esther and the King
10 min

Bible passage: Esther 4–5

Watch the video together, then work through the discussion questions below.

▶︎ Watch the Bible Story Video

The Story in Brief

Esther was a Jewish girl living in Persia who had become queen. A wicked man named Haman convinced the king to make a law that all Jewish people would be killed. Esther’s cousin Mordecai came to her and said, “Who knows? Maybe you were made queen for just such a time as this.” To save her people, Esther had to approach the king without being invited — something even the queen was not allowed to do. If the king held out his golden sceptre, she would be safe. If not, she could be put to death. Esther was scared. But she prayed, fasted, and trusted God — then did the brave thing anyway.

Discussion Questions

1. Why was Esther scared to go and see the king?
Sample answer: She wasn’t allowed to approach the king without being invited — even as queen. If the king didn’t hold out his golden sceptre, she could be put to death.
2. What did Esther do before she went to the king?
Sample answer: She prayed and fasted for three days — she didn’t eat while she asked God for help. She asked Mordecai and her friends to do the same. She trusted God before she acted.
3. What do you think “made for a time like this” means? Can you think of a way God might have a purpose for you?
Sample answer: It means God put Esther exactly where she was because He had a job for her to do. Accept any age-appropriate answers — being kind to a friend, being part of this church, helping a sibling.

Jesus Connection: Esther was brave because she trusted God’s plan — but she still had to face a scary moment herself. Jesus did something even braver: He knew exactly what was coming — the cross, the pain, the punishment for sin — and He went through it anyway. Not just to save one group of people, but to save everyone who trusts in Him. Just like Esther approached the king on behalf of her people, Jesus stands before God on behalf of us — and He already paid the price. He is the ultimate Brave Hero.

🙏 Prayer

Lord, thank you that you put us exactly where we are on purpose. Help us to trust you like Esther did — that when you call us to do something brave, you are already there with us. Thank you most of all for Jesus, the ultimate Brave Hero, who did the hardest, bravest thing of all so that we could be saved. Help us to remember that we are never alone, because you are always with us. Amen.

Craft – Esther’s Camera
10 min

Children colour, cut, and slide a film strip through a paper camera to retell the story of Esther. A hands-on way to bring the Bible story to life and take it home.

Before the Session

  • Print both pages onto A4 cardstock — one set per child.
  • Cut each printed sheet in half — each child gets one camera piece (top or bottom half of page 1) and one film strip (left or right half of page 2).
  • Cut the film strip slot on every camera along the dotted lines — a craft knife on a cutting mat gives the cleanest result.

Materials

  • Printed camera template — A4 cardstock, cut in half (one camera per child)
  • Printed film strip — A4 cardstock, cut in half (one strip per child)
  • Crayons or markers
  • Scissors
  • Craft knife or scissors for cutting the film slot (leader prep only)

Steps

  1. Hand out the camera and film strip pieces to each child.
  2. Children colour the camera and all five scenes on the film strip.
  3. Cut out the film strip along the outer edge.
  4. Slide the film strip through the slot in the camera.
Tip: As children slide through the scenes, ask them to narrate what’s happening in each picture. It’s a great way to recap the Bible story at the end of the session.
Leader note: Scroll through each scene with the children and ask them what’s happening in the picture — it’s a great low-pressure way to recap the Bible story. Use these descriptions to guide them:
  • Frame 1: Esther becomes queen.
  • Frame 2: Mordecai tells Esther about the plan to kill the Jews.
  • Frame 3: Esther bravely approaches the king.
  • Frame 4: Esther tells the king about Haman’s plan at the banquet.
  • Frame 5: The Jews celebrate the good news that they have been saved.
